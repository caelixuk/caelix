# Commercial licensing

CAELIX is released under the GNU Affero General Public License v3.0 or later (AGPL-3.0-or-later).

AGPL is appropriate for open research and public collaboration, but it may be incompatible with certain commercial deployments (for example: closed-source distribution, proprietary embedding, or hosted services where you cannot meet AGPL source-availability obligations).

If you require non-copyleft commercial terms, a separate commercial licence is available.

## Typical commercial use-cases

A commercial licence may be appropriate if you intend to:

- ship CAELIX (or a derivative) as part of a closed-source product
- embed CAELIX into a proprietary solver / pipeline (CPU/GPU, HPC)
- run a modified CAELIX as a hosted service/API without publishing corresponding source
- distribute binaries/containers to customers without AGPL obligations flowing through

## What a commercial licence can provide

Commercial terms are negotiated case-by-case, but typically cover:

- permission to use, modify, and distribute under non-copyleft terms
- permission for closed-source hosted services (SaaS/API deployments)
- optional attribution/branding clauses (e.g. “Powered by CAELIX”)
- optional support, integration, and performance work
